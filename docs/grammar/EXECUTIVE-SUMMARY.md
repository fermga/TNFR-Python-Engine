## What Does It Do?

The grammar system validates that computational sequences follow **five fundamental constraints** derived from TNFR physics:

### U1: Structural Initiation & Closure
**What it means**: Every simulation must have a proper beginning and ending.

**Business impact**: Prevents incomplete or undefined starting conditions that lead to crashes.

### U2: Convergence & Boundedness
**What it means**: Changes must be balanced - destabilization requires stabilization.

**Business impact**: Prevents runaway processes that consume resources or produce garbage results.

### U3: Resonant Coupling
**What it means**: Components can only connect if they're "in sync" (phase compatible).

**Business impact**: Prevents physically impossible connections that invalidate results.

### U4: Bifurcation Dynamics
**What it means**: Critical transitions need safety controls.

**Business impact**: Ensures system changes are controlled and predictable, not chaotic.

### U5: Multi-Scale Coherence
**What it means**: Hierarchical recursion (REMESH across scales) must be stabilized to conserve coherence across nested patterns.

**Business impact**: Prevents silent fragmentation in deeply nested simulations; protects model scalability and reliability.

## Glossary for Non-Technical Readers

**Grammar**: Rules that ensure correctness, like grammar in language

**Operator**: A basic action or transformation in the system

**Constraint**: A rule that must be followed (like "must start with capital letter")

**Validation**: Automatic checking that rules are followed

**Coherence**: How well parts work together as a unified whole

**Bifurcation**: A critical transition or change point in system behavior

**EPI**: The "form" or "structure" of a pattern (technical term from TNFR)

**Resonance**: When patterns align and reinforce each other

**Phase**: Timing or synchronization between components

**Sequence**: A series of operations applied in order
